Roam

Role Product Design Engineer
Timeline Jul – Dec 2025
Location Brooklyn, NY
Status Patent Pending (2025)

Overview

Roam is a Brooklyn-based consumer startup developing a product suite for on-the-go beverage carbonation. I joined as a Product Design Engineer co-op, embedded in a team of five engineers working to bring the product from pre-production prototypes to final manufacturing.

The work spanned the full product development cycle — pressure testing, iterative prototyping with 3D printed parts, DFM collaboration with overseas manufacturers, and ultimately contributing to a design that is now patent pending.

I led the design, prototyping, and validation of a seal solution that resolved a critical user experience failure discovered during pressure testing — carrying the design from initial research through final production implementation.

The Problem

During pressure testing, the team identified a recurring failure mode: water spray in over-pressurized scenarios that posed both a safety risk and a significant UX problem. No existing off-the-shelf seal addressed the specific geometry and pressure requirements of our system.

The challenge was to design a seal that would reliably prevent spray under edge-case pressures while remaining easy to operate in everyday use — without adding cost or complexity to the manufacturing process.

Process

I began with a research phase reviewing existing seal mechanisms and failure modes in analogous pressurized consumer products. From there I developed several geometric variants in SolidWorks, which were rapid-prototyped using the team's in-house 3D printers and tested against pressure chambers.

Each iteration was validated against safety thresholds and UX benchmarks. Testing procedures were developed and documented as part of the design validation — including protocols for daily-use wear and over-pressurization events.

Once a design direction was validated, I worked alongside the team in weekly calls with Chinese manufacturers to adapt the geometry for injection molding, managing DFM constraints through to final tooling.

Outcome

The seal design was adopted for production and a U.S. patent application was filed in 2025, with me listed as inventor. The product is currently progressing through final-stage manufacturing preparation.
